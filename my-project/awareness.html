Common UV Myths vs. Facts

"You can't get sunburnt on a cloudy day."

Tap to reveal

Up to 80% of UV rays can pass through clouds. It's the UV radiation, not the heat, that causes the burn.

"One application of SPF 50+ lasts all day."

Tap to reveal

Sunscreen wears off through sweating and friction. You must reapply every 2 hours to stay protected.

"If it's cool or windy, the sun won't burn you."

Tap to reveal

You can't feel UV radiation. Wind or cool temperatures don't reduce its intensity; it can be high even on cold days.

"There's SPF in my makeup, so I'm fully protected."

Tap to reveal

Most people don't apply enough makeup to reach the SPF rating. You still need proper sunscreen underneath.

"I can't get sun damage through glass windows."

Tap to reveal

While glass blocks most UVB rays, UVA rays (responsible for ageing) can still pass through plain glass.

"People with dark skin don't get skin cancer."

Tap to reveal

Everyone, regardless of skin type, is at risk of UV damage. Prevention is important for every skin tone.
